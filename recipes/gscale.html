Ginger Scales recipe

Ginger scales is how my fianc√© and I decided to call this dish. In essence, it's rice soaked in ginger, soy sauce, mirin, and sake; miso-marinaded salmon; and shocked salted spinach. We came up with the name, actually, because I didn't want to have a long file name for this project, haha.

Ingredients

Miso salmon

  • 4 skin-on salmon fillets
  • 2 Tbsp miso
  • 1 Tbsp sake
  • 1 Tbsp mirin (a Japanese cooking wine)
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce (I like Kikkoman)
  • 1/4 tsp roasted sesame oil

Ginger rice

  • 1 knob ginger
  • 1 1/3 cups water
  • 1 Tbps soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp sake
  • 1 Tbsp mirin
  • 1/4 tsp kosher salt

Salted spinach

  • 1 bag baby spinach
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 piece kombu
  • 1 Tbsp mirin
  • 1 Tbsp soysauce

Instructions

Cooking the miso salmon
  1. Assuming you have deboned and descaled your fish, begin by mixing the wet ingredients together (everything except the salmon) in a large bowl. Combine.
  2. Place the salmon into the bowl, skin-side up. Spoon some of the remaining marinade over the skin-side of the fish.
  3. Keep in the refrigerator for 1-2 hours maximum. Although it may be tempting to marinade for longer, the fish will become too salty and quite unpleasant to the taste -- 1-2 hours is ideal.
  4. Preheat oven to 425F with a rack lined with parchment paper.
  5. Remove marinade from fish. Miso burns easily, leaving you with a bitter, nasty taste if a large amount is left on any of the pieces of fish.
  6. Bake salmon for roughly 18-20 minutes, testing with an internal thermometer to ensure fish is cooked to a USDA recommended 120F.
  7. (Optional) Broil the salmon for 3-5 minutes, until a nice char forms.
  8. The marinade is definitely unusable now; discard.
Cooking the ginger rice

I recommend preparing and cooking the ginger rice whilst the salmon is marinading, particularly if you're using a rice cooker. A rice cooker will keep your rice hot until ready to plate -- there's also no harm in preparing the ginger rice first, except that the aroma is so delightful that you'll want to dig in sooner.

  1. Rinse and drain your rice well.
  2. Peel ginger and julienne into small strips.
  3. Mix soy, mirin, sake, in a measuring cup (I usually use my Pyrex here). Add water so that the total liquid is around 1.5 cups of liquid. Add salt, and mix very well.
  4. Put your drained rice into the rice cooker. Add the liquid from your Pyrex into the rice cooker and do not stir.
  5. Let rice soak for about 20 minutes.
  6. Cook rice on "Mixed rice" option if available. If none is available, cook for a few minutes longer on a regular setting.
  7. Once your rice cooker has sung its little melody to let you know the rice is finished, let sit for another 10 minutes before fluffing with paddle.
Cooking the spinach
  1. Put your water and kombu into a saucepan.
  2. Slowly bring to boil. Once boiling, remove kombu.
  3. Add mirin and soy sauce to the water, and mix. Turn off heat once boiling.
  4. Rinse and clean spinach. Boil a large pot of water.
  5. Once the water is boiling, add salt. Put the spinach into the pot, and cook for 2 minutes.
  6. Transfer spinach to ice bath. Once cooled, collect spinach on paper towel and squeeze dry/pat dry.
  7. Put spinach into airtight container and pour the sauce (with the soy and mirin) over the spinach.
  8. Let soak in refrigerator, roughly 1 hour. If you're impatient, it'll still taste pretty good earlier. :-)
To serve
  • Spoon some rice onto plate in attractive fashion. Add a piece of salmon. Finally, add some of your spinach. You can warm the spinach up in the microwave if you prefer.
