Lentil soup with tomatoes

The basis for this recipe is from Cookie and Kate's blog. I cannot take any credit for this recipe, but I WILL say that I definitely vouch that this is, in fact, the greatest lentil soup out there!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of green or brown lentils, picked over for duds
  • 1 can of low- or no-sodium tomatoes, lightly drained
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 6 cloves garlic (to taste -- some of us enjoy the taste coming through)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 carrots
  • 2 stalks celery (optional -- I don't add myself, but to complete the mirepoix, will add nice taste)
  • 4 cups low- or no-sodium vegetable broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 teaspoons cumin
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garam massala OR curry powder. Note, the Japanese curry roux from my last recipe will NOT work as a substitute here
  • Salt and pepper to taste

How to make

  1. Slice and dice your onion and celery, set aside
  2. Peel and chop carrots, set aside
  3. Use a garlic grater or press garlic, set aside
  4. I usually take this time to do a few menial tasks to facilitate the rest of the cooking process here: I measure out my spices, I use a can opener to open the can of tomatoes. You can also separate the kale from the stems and roughly chop at this stage, although the kale will be used at a later time.
  5. Once the oil is shimmering, add onions, celery, and carrots. Stir regularly.
  6. When onions are translucent and carrots have softened, add garlic, cumin, oregano, and garam massala/curry. Stir constantly until aromatic (about 30 seconds).
  7. Add canned tomatoes, continuing to stir.
  8. Pour in lentils, broth, and water. Add 1 tsp salt and pepper.
  9. Bring to a boil, then reduce to simmering. Cook for 30 minutes with the pot partially covered.
  10. Transfer 2 cups soup to a blender. Cover top of blender with a tea towel/small clean cooking rag, and blend until pureed. Return soup to pot, integrate.
  11. Add chopped dino kale, cook for another 5 minutes.
  12. Add salt, pepper, lemon, whatever you like, to taste.

Usually when the soup is in the pot cooking away at this state, I have to resist the urge to gobble it up right then!
