Japanese Curry

Japanese Curry -- easy to make at home!

Ingredients

  • One box Japanese curry roux. (When I'm feeling lazy, I use Japanese curry roux from the box, but JustOneCookbook has a great homemade Japanese curry roux recipe!)
  • 2 large carrots
  • Potatoes (Note: I use 6 small red potatoes, Yukon will do)
  • 2 onions
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 small apple
  • 1 knob ginger
  • 1 Tbsp soysauce (Note: I like Kikkoman)
  • 1 Tbsp ketchup
  • 1 Tbsp honey
  • 4 cups low- or no-sodium vegetable stock (or chicken/beef)
  • 1.5 Tbsp neutral oil (I usually use a spray-on oil like Pam, but if you don't have one, any neutral tasting oil will do)
  • Frozen seafood mix -- I like Trader Joe's brand, any non-fatty fish will do
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Japanese short-grain rice (according to how much curry you'll eat)

How to cook

  1. Rinse 1-2 rice-cooker-cups rice, and let sit for about 20 minutes. Then cook per manufacturer's instructions.
  2. Cut onions in half, then, with the cut-side on the cutting board, slice vertically. You should have long pieces of onion. Set aside.
  3. Scrub and peel potatoes and carrots. Cut potatoes in half. For the carrots, I use the rangiri method outlined on JustOneCookbook's website. Set aside.
  4. Peel the apple, and cut in half. Core it. Grate the apple using a grater, or finely chop (the apple will mostly dissolve in the curry anyway). Set aside.
  5. Use either a garlic press or a garlic grater for the garlic and ginger. Set aside.
  6. Using a large pot over medium heat, add your oil (or spray with Pam).
  7. Once the oil is shimmering nicely, add the onion. Cook the onion down until it just begins to caramelize.
  8. Add the grated garlic and grated ginger to the onions.
  9. Add the frozen seafood bag (as-is) to the pot. Cook until you can see the color return in the shrimp.
  10. Add the veggie stock.
  11. Add your grated or chopped apple, ketchup, honey, and soy sauce. My Japanese students told me that instant coffee was also a nice addition to curry. Some also liked chocolate (dark, of course).
  12. Add the carrots and potatoes.
  13. Simmer, with the pot covered, for about twenty minutes.
  14. When you can pierce a potato effortlessly, you can begin adding the curry roux.
  15. Turn off the heat, and put a block of the curry roux in a cooking spoon. Ladle the liquid into the spoon, and using a fork or a pair of chopsticks, mush up the roux block so that it's integrated. Repeat for the entire box of the curry roux.
  16. Turn heat back on, and simmer unconvered for about ten minutes.
  17. Test the curry at this point. Add salt to taste (for me, roughly 2 tsp).
Serving
  1. Serve curry with steamed rice.

Japanese curry is a favorite among many Japanese families, and tourists who come to Japan! In fact, it's so popular, that when I make it, my cat, Helga, looks on jealously as I won't give her any :-)
